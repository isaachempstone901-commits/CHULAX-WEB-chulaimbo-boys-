Establishment and Growth

Chulaimbo Boys High School was established in 1965 as a mixed secondary school to serve the growing demand for secondary education in the western region of Kenya. During that time, many students in rural areas had limited access to quality post-primary education, and local community leaders, together with the government and missionary influence in the area, supported the creation of the school. It began with a small number of students, limited classrooms, and basic facilities. In its early years, the school operated as a day and boarding mixed institution, offering education to both boys and girls from Chulaimbo and surrounding areas such as Maseno and Kisumu West. As enrollment increased and the need for improved academic performance grew, education stakeholders reviewed its structure. The decision was later made to convert it into a boys-only boarding school to enhance discipline, academic concentration, and structured learning. Following its transition, the school expanded its infrastructure, including dormitories, science laboratories, and administrative buildings. Over the years, it steadily built a reputation for academic improvement and holistic development. Today, Chulaimbo Boys High School stands as one of the respected public secondary schools in Kisumu County, continuing its mission of providing quality education and nurturing responsible young men.
