Academic Excellence

The school follows the Kenyan secondary school curriculum under the Ministry of Education. It consistently performs well in national examinations (KCSE) and is known for discipline and academic competitiveness.

Students are encouraged to develop holistically, emphasizing integrity, hard work, and personal responsibility.

Chulaimbo Boys High School has maintained a strong academic presence in Kisumu County over the last three years (2023–2025), consistently achieving mean grades above 7.0 (C+) and producing top national candidates. In the 2025 KCSE examinations, the school recorded a mean score of 7.0329 (C+), with Adem Stephen Packham emerging as a top national student, achieving a straight A (84 points). In 2024, the school attained a mean score of 8.1861 (B-), with an impressive university transition rate of 91.8% (529 out of 576 candidates) qualifying for direct entry. The 2023 results saw a mean score of 6.592 (C+), with 144 students qualifying for university and the school ranking among the top nationally. Key developments at Chulaimbo Boys High School include its continued recognition as a "Center of Excellence" in Kisumu West Sub-County, frequently ranking among the top three schools regionally. Enrollment has grown to over 1,250 boys, supported by a teaching staff of approximately 22 to 30 educators. As of January 2026, the school has successfully transitioned to the Competency-Based Education (CBE) system for Grade 10 (Senior School), offering pathways in STEM, Social Sciences, and Arts & Sports. The school’s consistent performance has been highlighted in local media, including The Kenya Times and on Facebook, showcasing its strong academic results and achievements of top-performing students such as Stephen Packham Adem. Chulaimbo Boys continues to stand out as a leading institution in Kisumu County, combining academic excellence with holistic student development.
